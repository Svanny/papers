SCORES: Shelf Trajectories with Slow and Fast Components via Spectral Quadratic Surrogates

A two time scale formulation for online reordering while tracking a drifting physical shelf layout.

Read paper Open PDF Download PDF View TeX View BibTeX
AuthorMonysovann Ly
DateFebruary 19, 2026
FormatPDF + TeX + BibTeX
LicenseCC BY 4.0

Abstract

We study online reordering of a finite set of items to reduce user navigation time while remaining consistent with a drifting physical shelf layout. Raw swipe adjacency is a policy artifact, so the paper proposes learning proximity only from consecutive update events.

To obtain a solver with clean spectral structure, the paper replaces absolute distance costs by a Laplacian quadratic surrogate that diagonalizes in the graph Fourier basis. It then formulates a two time scale trajectory problem, with a slow shelf component and a fast app component coupled by a proximity term and controlled by temporal smoothness.

Keywords

Online reordering, spectral seriation, Laplacian quadratic surrogate, time frequency smoothing, drifting shelf inference, constrained swaps.
