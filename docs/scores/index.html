Dynamic Latent Order Inference for Session Editing with Cold Item Uncertainty

A Bayesian state-space formulation for dynamic ordering from edit-session traces with cold-item uncertainty.

Abstract

This paper studies the problem of inferring a user preferred ordering of a finite set of items from interaction traces generated by edit sessions. The preferred order is treated as a drifting hidden state.

Observations are noisy and partial, and many items are cold in the sense that they receive updates infrequently. The paper proposes a state-space formulation in which latent one dimensional coordinates induce a permutation by sorting, while inactivity increases posterior uncertainty rather than enforcing a deterministic freeze.

Keywords

Dynamic ranking, state space models, Laplace approximation, Poisson likelihood, cold start, uncertainty inflation, conservative ordering.
